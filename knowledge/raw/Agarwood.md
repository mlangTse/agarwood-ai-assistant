**Agarwood**, **aloeswood**, **eaglewood**, **gaharuwood**, commonly referred to as **oud** or **oudh.** First referenced in the Old Testament of the Bible and referred to as Aloes. Ref. Old Testament: Numbers 24:6: Compares the beauty and prosperity of the tents of Israel to 'aloes planted by the Lord." (from [Hebrew:](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Hebrew_language "Hebrew language")*'ahalim'* or *'ahaloth'*, [Arabic](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Arabic_language "Arabic language"): عود, [romanized](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Romanization_of_Arabic "Romanization of Arabic"): *ʿūd*, pronounced [\[ʕuːd\]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Help:IPA/Arabic "Help:IPA/Arabic")), is a fragrant, dark and [resinous](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Resin "Resin") [wood](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Wood "Wood") used in [incense](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Incense "Incense"), perfume, and small [hand carvings](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Woodworking).  
沉香木，也被称为 oud 或 oudh。在《圣经》的旧约中首次被提及，当时被称为“Aloes”。参考《旧约·民数记》24:6：“将以色列的帐篷与‘由耶和华种植的沉香木’进行比较。”（源自希伯来语： ' ahalim ' 或 ' ahaloth ' ；阿拉伯语：عود，罗马化：ʿūd，发音为 [\[ʕuːd\]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Help:IPA/Arabic "Help:IPA/Arabic") ）。沉香木是一种芳香浓郁、带有树脂的木材，常用于制作香烛、香水以及小型手工艺品。

It forms in the [heartwood](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Heartwood "Heartwood") of *[Aquilaria](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Aquilaria "Aquilaria")* trees after they become infected with a type of *[Phaeoacremonium](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Phaeoacremonium "Phaeoacremonium")* mold, *P. parasitica*. The tree defensively secretes a resin to combat the fungal infestation. Prior to becoming infected, the heartwood mostly lacks scent, and is relatively light and pale in colouration. However, as the infection advances and the tree produces its fragrant resin as a final option of defense, the heartwood becomes very dense, dark, and saturated with resin. This product is harvested, and most famously referred to in [cosmetics](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Cosmetics "Cosmetics") under the scent names of *oud*, *oodh* or *aguru*; however, it is also called *aloes* (not to be confused with the [succulent plant](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Succulent_plant "Succulent plant") [genus](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Genus "Genus") *[Aloe](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Aloe "Aloe")*), *agar* (this name, as well, is not to be confused with the edible, [algae](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Algae "Algae") -derived thickening agent [agar agar](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Agar_agar "Agar agar")), as well as *gaharu* or *jinko*. With thousands of years of known use, and valued across [Hindu](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Hinduism "Hinduism"), [Buddhist](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Buddhism "Buddhism"), [Muslim](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Islam "Islam") and [Chinese](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Chinese_people "Chinese people") cultures, oud is prized in Middle Eastern and South Asian cultures for its distinctive fragrance, utilized in [colognes](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Colognes "Colognes"), [incense](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Incense "Incense") and [perfumes](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Perfumes "Perfumes").  
这种树脂形成于 Aquilaria 树的木材中，当这些树木受到 Phaeoacremonium 霉菌 P. parasitica 的感染之后。为了抵御这种真菌侵害，树木会分泌树脂作为防御机制。在感染发生之前，木材通常没有香味，颜色也较为浅淡。然而，随着感染的发展，树木会分泌出芳香的树脂作为最后的防御手段，此时木材变得非常密集、颜色深暗，并充满了树脂。这种树脂被采集后，在化妆品中被用作各种香味名称，如 oud、oodh 或 aguru。不过，它也被称为 aloes（不要与含羞草属的植物混淆），agar（这个名称同样不要与可食用的、源自藻类的增稠剂 agar agar 混淆），还有 gaharu 或 jinko。奥德木历经数千年使用，在印度教、佛教、伊斯兰教和中华文化中备受推崇。它在中东和南亚文化中同样受到珍视，因其独特的香气而被用于制作香水、香膏等物品。

![](https://upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/f/f9/Agarwood.jpg/250px-Agarwood.jpg)

Uninfected aquilaria wood lacking the dark resin 未受感染的 Aquilaria 木材，不含深色树脂

One of the main reasons for the relative rarity and high cost of agarwood is the depletion of wild sources.[^1] Since 1995, the [Convention on International Trade in Endangered Species of Wild Fauna and Flora](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/CITES "CITES") has listed *[Aquilaria malaccensis](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Aquilaria_malaccensis "Aquilaria malaccensis")* (the primary source) in its [Appendix II (potentially threatened species)](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Appendix_II "Appendix II").[^2] In 2004, all *Aquilaria* species were listed in Appendix II; however, a number of countries have outstanding reservations regarding that listing.[^2]  
琼脂木之所以相对稀有且价格昂贵，其中一个主要原因是野生资源的枯竭。自 1995 年以来，《濒危野生动植物国际贸易公约》将 Aquilaria malaccensis 列为附录 II 中的受威胁物种。2004 年时，所有琼脂木属物种都被列入了附录 II；不过，一些国家对这一名录持有强烈的保留意见。

The varying aromatic qualities of agarwood are influenced by the species, geographic location, its branch, trunk and root origin, length of time since infection, and methods of harvesting and processing.[^3] Agarwood is one of the most expensive woods in the world, along with [African blackwood](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Dalbergia_melanoxylon "Dalbergia melanoxylon"), [sandalwood](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Sandalwood "Sandalwood"), [pink ivory](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Pink_ivory "Pink ivory") and [ebony](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Ebony "Ebony").[^4] First-grade agarwood is one of the most expensive natural raw materials in the world,[^5] with the year of 2026 prices for superior pure material are as high as $ 290,000 US/kg ~ € 250,000 Euro/kg, although in practice adulteration of the wood and oil is common, allowing for prices as low as US$100/kg for artificial (Inocculated), plantation or underage harvested (unriped to its fullest potential).[^6] A wide range of qualities and products come to market, varying in quality with geographical location, botanical species, the age of the specific tree, cultural deposition and the section of the tree where the piece of agarwood stems from.[^7]  
琼脂木的香气特性受到其来源、地理位置、树枝、树干及根部来源、感染后的时间长短以及采集和加工方法的影响。琼脂木是世界上最昂贵的木材之一，与非洲黑木、檀香木、粉红象牙木和乌木齐名。一级琼脂木是世界上最昂贵的天然原材料之一，2026 年时，优质纯料的价格高达每公斤 29.000 美元，即 250,000 欧元/公斤。不过实际上，这种木材和树脂常常被掺假，因此低价仿制品的价格可能低至每公斤 100 美元，这些产品通常来自人工培育的树木或未成年树木的木材。市场上流通的琼脂木产品种类繁多，其品质因地理位置、植物种类、树木的年龄等因素而有所不同。文化的传承方式，以及那棵树的特定部位，也就是那块沉香的来源之处。 [^7]

## History 历史

The odour of agarwood is complex and pleasing,[^8] with few or no similar natural analogues. In the perfume state, the scent is mainly distinguished by a combination of "oriental-woody" and "very soft fruity-floral" notes. The incense smoke is also characterised by a "sweet-balsamic" note and "shades of vanilla and musk" and amber.[^7] As a result, agarwood and its [essential oil](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Essential_oil "Essential oil") gained great cultural and religious significance in ancient civilizations around the world. Over 3,000 years ago, the [ancient Egyptians](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Ancient_Egyptians "Ancient Egyptians") used agarwood incense in their funeral rites.[^9] It was described as a fragrant product as early as 1400 BCE in the [Vedas](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Vedas "Vedas") of [India](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/India "India").[^10]  
沉香的气味十分复杂且令人愉悦，几乎没有类似的自然物质可以与之相媲美。在香水领域，沉香的气味主要呈现出“东方木质味”与“非常柔和的水果香和花香”这两种特质。而沉香烟则具有“甜美的香脂味”，同时带有香草和麝香的味道，还有琥珀的香气。因此，沉香及其精油在世界各地的古代文明中具有重要的文化和宗教意义。早在 3000 多年前，古埃及人在葬礼仪式中就会使用沉香香熏。早在公元前 1400 年，印度《吠陀经》中就已经描述了沉香是一种芳香的制品。

In the [Hebrew Bible](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Hebrew_Bible "Hebrew Bible"), "trees of lign aloes" are mentioned in [The Book of Numbers](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/The_Book_of_Numbers "The Book of Numbers") 24:6 [^11] and a perfume compounded of aloeswood, [myrrh](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Myrrh "Myrrh"), and [cassia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Cassia_bark "Cassia bark") is described in Psalms 45.[^12]  
在希伯来圣经中，《民数记》24:6 提到了“木柱树”的情节；而在《诗篇》45 中则描述了一种由香木、没药和桂香混合而成的香膏。 [^12]

In the [Gospel of John](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Gospel_of_John "Gospel of John"), [Jesus](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Jesus "Jesus") ’s body is prepared for burial by binding it in linen wrappings with seventy-five pounds of aloes and [myrrh](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Myrrh "Myrrh") (John 19:39).  
在《约翰福音》中，耶稣的身体被用亚麻布包裹起来，并加上了 75 磅的乳香和没药，以便进行安葬（约翰福音 19:39）。

![](https://upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/0/0f/Qing-Agarwood-Rosary.jpg/250px-Qing-Agarwood-Rosary.jpg)

Antique agarwood beads with inlaid gold, late Qing dynasty, China. Adilnor Collection, Sweden. 古老的 Agarwood 珠子，镶有金饰，源自中国清朝晚期。瑞典 Adilnor 收藏。

[Dioscorides](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Dioscorides "Dioscorides") in his book *[De materia medica](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/De_materia_medica "De materia medica")* (50 to 70 CE) described several medical qualities of agarwood (Áγαλλοχου) and mentioned its use as an incense. Even though Dioscorides describes agarwood as having an astringent and bitter taste, it was used to freshen the breath when chewed or as a decoction held in the mouth. He also writes that a root extract was used to treat stomach complaints and dysentery as well as pains of the lungs and liver.[^3] Agarwood's use as a medicinal product was also recorded in the [Sahih Muslim](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Sahih_Muslim "Sahih Muslim"), which dates back to approximately the ninth century, and in the [Ayurvedic](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Ayurvedic "Ayurvedic") medicinal text the [Susruta Samhita](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Susruta_Samhita "Susruta Samhita").[^13]  
在《药物论》一书中，迪奥斯科里德斯描述了琥珀的多种药用特性，并提到将其用作香料的用途。尽管迪奥斯科里德斯认为琥珀具有收敛和苦味，但人们还是会通过咀嚼或将其制成汤剂来清新口气。他还写道，琥珀的提取物被用于治疗胃部疾病、痢疾以及肺部和肝脏的疼痛问题。关于琥珀作为药物使用的记载，也可以出现在《穆斯林圣训集》中，该文献的成书时间大约在九世纪左右；此外，在印度医学经典《苏尔塔萨姆希塔》中也提到了琥珀的药用价值。

As early as the third century CE in ancient Viet Nam, the Chinese [chronicle](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Chronicle "Chronicle") *Nan zhou yi wu zhi* (Strange things from the South) written by Wa Zhen of the Eastern [Wu Dynasty](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Wu_\(state\) "Wu (state)") mentioned agarwood produced in the Rinan commandery, now Central [Vietnam](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Vietnam "Vietnam"), and how people collected it in the mountains. During the sixth century CE in Japan, in the recordings of the *[Nihon Shoki](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Nihon_Shoki "Nihon Shoki")* (The Chronicles of Japan) the second oldest book of classical Japanese history, mention is made of a large piece of fragrant wood identified as agarwood. The source for this piece of wood is claimed to be from [Pursat, Cambodia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Pursat_Province "Pursat Province") (based on the smell of the wood). The [famous piece of wood](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Ranjatai_\(wood\) "Ranjatai (wood)") still remains in Japan today and is showcased less than 10 times per century at the [Nara National Museum](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Nara_National_Museum "Nara National Museum").  
早在公元 3 世纪的古代越南，东吴时期의 왕진所撰写的《南州异物志》一书中提到了位于今中越地区的临安郡出产的沉香。而在公元 6 世纪的日本，日本最古老的史书《日本纪》中也提到了一种被称为沉香的珍贵木材。据记载，这种木材的来源地是柬埔寨的普尔萨特——这是根据木材的气味判断出来的。至今，这种珍贵的木材仍然保存在日本，并且每世纪只有不到十次在奈良国立博物馆展出。

![](https://upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/c/cd/RanJyaTai_Shosoin.JPG/250px-RanJyaTai_Shosoin.JPG)

Ranjatai, 8th century, currently held by the Shōsōin, Tōdai-ji Nara, Japan 兰贾泰，8 世纪时期，目前由正兴寺掌管，位于日本奈良的东大寺

Agarwood is highly revered in Hinduism, Buddhism, and [Chinese folk religion](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Chinese_folk_religion "Chinese folk religion").[^3] [^14]  
白檀在印度教、佛教以及中国民间宗教中备受推崇。 [^3] [^14]

Starting in 1580 after [Nguyễn Hoàng](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Nguy%E1%BB%85n_Ho%C3%A0ng "Nguyễn Hoàng") took control over the central provinces of modern Vietnam, he encouraged trade with other countries, specifically China and Japan. Agarwood was exported in three varieties: Calambac (kỳ nam in Vietnamese), trầm hương (very similar but slightly harder and slightly more abundant), and agarwood proper. A pound of Calambac bought in [Hội An](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/H%E1%BB%99i_An_\(city\) "Hội An (city)") for 15 [taels](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Taels "Taels") could be sold in [Nagasaki](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Nagasaki,_Nagasaki "Nagasaki, Nagasaki") for 600 taels. The [Nguyễn Lords](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Nguy%E1%BB%85n_Lords "Nguyễn Lords") soon established a [Royal Monopoly](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Legal_monopoly "Legal monopoly") over the sale of Calambac. This monopoly helped fund the Nguyễn state finances during the early years of the Nguyen rule.[^15] Accounts of international trade in agarwood date back as early as the thirteenth century, with India being one of the earliest sources of agarwood for foreign markets.[^16]  
自 1580 年起，当阮潢掌控了现代越南的中部省份后，他鼓励与其他国家进行贸易往来，尤其是与中国和日本的贸易。沉香有三种不同的品种：Calambac（在越南语中称为“ kỳ nam”），trầm hương（与前者非常相似，但品质稍好且产量稍多），以及真正的沉香。在会安购买一磅 Calambac，价值 15 两银币，而在长崎则可以卖出 600 两银币。阮朝的统治者很快建立了对 Calambac 销售的垄断权。这一垄断权在阮朝统治初期为国家的财政提供了支持。关于沉香国际贸易的记载可以追溯到 13 世纪，当时印度是最早将沉香出口到国外市场的来源地之一。

[Xuanzang](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Xuanzang "Xuanzang") 's travelogues and the *[Harshacharita](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Harshacharita "Harshacharita")*, written in seventh century AD in Northern India, mentions use of agarwood products such as 'Xasipat' (writing-material) and 'aloe-oil' in ancient [Assam](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Assam "Assam") (Kamarupa). The tradition of making writing materials from its bark still exists in [Assam](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Assam "Assam"). It is still used in traditional Chinese [herbal medicine](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Herbal_medicine "Herbal medicine") where it goes by the name of Chén Xiāng - 沉香 - Literally meaning 'sinking fragrance'. Its earliest recorded mention is from the Miscellaneous Records of Famous Physicians, 名医别录, Ming Yi Bie Lu, ascribed to the author Táo Hǒng-Jǐng c.420-589.[^17]

## Etymology

The word Agar ultimately comes from one of the [Dravidian](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Dravidian_languages "Dravidian languages") languages,[^18] [^19] probably from Tamil அகில் (aghil).[^20]

### Vernacular names

![](https://upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/5/50/Aquilaria_crassna.jpg/250px-Aquilaria_crassna.jpg)

Aquilaria tree showing darker agarwood. Poachers had scraped off the bark to allow the tree to become infected by the ascomycetous mould.

Agarwood is known under many names in different cultures:[^21] [^22]

- Another name is *Lignum aloes* or Aloeswood, unrelated to the familiar genus, *[Aloe](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Aloe "Aloe")*. Also from *akil*, via Hebrew and Greek.[^23]
- In [Assamese](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Assamese_language "Assamese language"), it is called *xasi* (সাঁচি).[^24]
- In [Bengali](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Bengali_language "Bengali language"), it is commonly known by the same Sanskrit name as Aguru. The agarwood is known as *agor gach (আগর গাছ)* and the agarwood oil as *agor ator (আগর আতর)*.
- In [Odia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Odia_language "Odia language"), it is called *agara* (ଅଗର).
- In [Malayalam](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Malayalam_language "Malayalam language"), it is called *akil* (അകിൽ).
- In Cambodia, it is called *chann crassna*. The fragrance from this wood is called *khloem chann* (ខ្លឹមចាន់) or *khloem chann crassna*. *khloem* is 'hard wood' and *chann crassna* is the tree species *Aquilaria crassna* in the Khmer language.
- In [Hindi](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Hindustani_language "Hindustani language") it is known as *agar*, which is derived originally from the [Sanskrit](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Sanskrit "Sanskrit") *aguru*.[^25] [^26]
- In [Sinhala](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Sinhalese_language "Sinhalese language"), agarwood-producing *[Gyrinops walla](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Gyrinops_walla "Gyrinops walla")* tree is known as "Walla Patta" (වල්ල පට්ට).
- In [Tamil](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Tamil_language "Tamil language"), it is called *aghil* (அகில்) though what was referred in ancient Tamil literature could well be *Excoecaria agallocha*.
- In [Telugu](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Telugu_language "Telugu language") and [Kannada](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Kannada "Kannada"), it is known by the same Sanskrit name as *Aguru*.
- It is known as *Chénxiāng* (沉香) in [Chinese](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Chinese_language "Chinese language"), *Chimhyang* (침향) in [Korean](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Korean_language "Korean language"), *Jinkō* (沈香) in [Japanese](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Japanese_language "Japanese language"), and *trầm hương* in [Vietnamese](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Vietnamese_language "Vietnamese language"); all meaning "deep scent" and alluding to its intense scent. In Japan, there are several grades of *Jinkō*, the highest of which is known as *Kyara* (伽羅).[^27] In [Vietnam](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Vietnam "Vietnam"), ancient texts also refer to the use of agarwood in relation to travelling Buddhist monks.[^28]
- In [Tibetan](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Standard_Tibetan "Standard Tibetan") it is known as ཨ་ག་རུ་ (a-ga-ru). There are several varieties used in [Tibetan Medicine](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Tibetan_Medicine "Tibetan Medicine"): unique eaglewood, yellow eaglewood: ཨ་ག་རུ་སེར་པོ་ (a-ga-ru ser-po), white eaglewood: ཨར་སྐྱ་ (ar-skya), and black eaglewood: ཨར་ནག་(ar-nag).[^29] \\
- Both agarwood and its resin distillate/extracts are known as *oud* (عود) in [Arabic](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Arabic_language "Arabic language") (literally "rod/stick") and used to describe agarwood in Arab countries.[^30] Western perfumers also often use agarwood essential oil under the name "oud" or "oudh".[^31]
- In English-speaking Europe, it was referred to as *Lignum aquila* (eagle-wood) or *Agilawood*, from similarity to Tamil-Malayalam *aghil*.
- In [Indonesian](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Indonesian_language "Indonesian language") and [Malay](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Malay_language "Malay language"), it is called *gaharu*.
- In the [Philippines](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Philippines "Philippines"), it is known as *lapnisan*.
- In [Papua New Guinea](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Papua_New_Guinea "Papua New Guinea"), it is called "ghara" or eagle wood.
- In [Thai](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Thai_language "Thai language"), it is known as *mai kritsana* (ไม้กฤษณา).[^32]
- In [Laos](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Laos "Laos"), it is known as *mai ketsana* (ໄມ້ເກດສະໜາ).[^33]
- In Myanmar ([Burmese](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Burmese_language "Burmese language")), it is known as *Thit Mhwae* (သစ်မွှေး).

## Formation

### Production mode

There are seventeen species in the genus *[Aquilaria](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Aquilaria "Aquilaria")*, large evergreens native to [southeast Asia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Southeast_Asia "Southeast Asia") and [south asia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/South_asia "South asia"), of which nine are known to produce agar wood.[^34] Agarwood can in theory be produced from all members, but until recently it was primarily produced from *A. malaccensis* (*A. agallocha* and *A. secundaria* are synonyms for *A. malaccensis*).[^1] *A. crassna* and *A. sinensis* are the other two members of the genus that are commonly harvested. The [gyrinops](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Gyrinops "Gyrinops") tree can also produce agarwood.[^35] [^36]

![](https://upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/6/68/Condensers.jpg/250px-Condensers.jpg)

Steam distillation process used to extract agarwood essential oils

Long term Agar wood forms in the trunk and roots of trees that have been penetrated by an [Ambrosia beetle](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Ambrosia_beetle "Ambrosia beetle") insect the *Dinoplatypus chevrolati* first discovered by Stephan Alexander Peter, feeding on wood and oily resin. The tree may then be infected by a mould, and in response it produces a salutary self-defence material to conceal damages or infections. While the unaffected wood of the tree is relatively light in colour, the resin dramatically increases the mass and density of the affected wood, changing its colour from a pale beige to yellow, orange, red, dark brown or black. In natural forests, only about 7 out of 100 Aquilaria trees of the same species are infected and produce aloes/agar wood. A common method in planted forestry is to [inoculate](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Inoculate "Inoculate") trees with the fungus. It produces a "damage sap" and is referred to as "fake" aloes/agar wood.[^34] [^37]

Oud oil can be distilled from agar wood using steam; the total yield of oil for 70 kg of wood will not exceed 20 ml.[^38] [^39]

### Composition

The composition of agarwood oil is exceedingly complex with more than 150 chemical compounds identified.[^6] At least 70 of these are [terpenoids](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Terpenoids "Terpenoids") which come in the form of [sesquiterpenes](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Sesquiterpenes "Sesquiterpenes") and [chromones](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Chromones "Chromones"); no [monoterpenes](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Monoterpenes "Monoterpenes") have been detected at all. Other common classes of compounds include [agarofurans](https://en.wikipedia.org/w/index.php?title=Agarofuran&action=edit&redlink=1 "Agarofuran (page does not exist)"), [cadinanes](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Cadalene "Cadalene"), [eudesmanes](https://en.wikipedia.org/w/index.php?title=Eudesmane&action=edit&redlink=1 "Eudesmane (page does not exist)"), [valencanes](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Valencene "Valencene") and [eremophilanes](https://en.wikipedia.org/w/index.php?title=Eremophilane&action=edit&redlink=1 "Eremophilane (page does not exist)"), [guaianes](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Guaiene "Guaiene"), [prezizanes](https://en.wikipedia.org/w/index.php?title=Prezizane&action=edit&redlink=1 "Prezizane (page does not exist)"), [vetispiranes](https://en.wikipedia.org/w/index.php?title=Vetispirane&action=edit&redlink=1 "Vetispirane (page does not exist)"), simple volatile aromatic compounds as well as a range of miscellaneous compounds.[^6] The exact balance of these materials will vary depending on the age and species of tree as well as the exact details of the oil extraction process.

## Perfumery

Oud has become a popular component in perfumery. Most brands have a creation based on or dedicated to "oud" or an accord of oud created through the use of certain chemical scent components. Few perfume houses use real oud in their creations. This is because oud is very expensive and potent. Oud is generally used as a base note and is traditionally paired with rose. Oud essential oil is available on the internet but care should be taken in choosing the vendor. Due to the fact that oud is such an expensive material there is a big market for diluting oud oil with patchouli or other chemical components.

Oud scent is popular in the [Middle East](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Middle_East "Middle East"), the [Arab world](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Arab_world "Arab world"), and in [Arab culture](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Arab_culture "Arab culture"), where it is used as a traditional aromatic and perfume in many forms. Oud is also one of the reasons why the Arab region developed trade routes in ancient times. Popular amongst [Muslims](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Muslims "Muslims"), it has been traditionally used in [Mosques](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Mosques "Mosques") where the incense chips are burned.[^40]

The aroma of agarwood oil is described as sweet, (characteristically) agarwood, woody, balsamic, sandalwood, leathery, fruity, smoky, animal, and tobacco.[^41]

## Conservation of agarwood-producing species

[Overharvesting](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Overharvesting "Overharvesting") and [habitat loss](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Habitat_loss "Habitat loss") threatens some populations of agarwood-producing species. Concern over the impact of the global demand for agarwood has thus led to the inclusion of the main taxa on [CITES](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/CITES "CITES") Appendix II, which requires that international trade in agarwood be monitored. Monitoring is conducted by Cambridge-based [TRAFFIC](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Traffic_\(conservation_programme\) "Traffic (conservation programme)") (a joint [WWF](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/World_Wide_Fund_for_Nature "World Wide Fund for Nature") and [IUCN](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/International_Union_for_Conservation_of_Nature "International Union for Conservation of Nature") programme).[^44] CITES also provides that international trade in agarwood be subject to controls designed to ensure that harvest and exports are not to the detriment of the survival of the species in the wild.[^45]

In addition, agarwood plantations have been established in a number of countries like India, Bangladesh, Bhutan and reintroduced into countries such as Malaysia and Sri Lanka as commercial plantation crops.[^44] [^46] [^47] The success of these plantations depends on the stimulation of agarwood production in the trees. Numerous inoculation techniques have been developed, with varying degrees of success.[^34]
