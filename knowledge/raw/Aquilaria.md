***Aquilaria*** is a genus of trees, called **lign aloes** or **lign-aloes trees**, in the family [Thymelaeaceae](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Thymelaeaceae "Thymelaeaceae"). It includes 21 species native to southeast Asia. They occur particularly in the [rainforests](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Rainforest "Rainforest") of Indonesia, Thailand, Cambodia, Laos, Vietnam, southern China, Malaysia, [Northeast India](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Northeast_India "Northeast India"), Bangladesh, the Philippines, [Borneo](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Borneo "Borneo") and New Guinea.[^1] The trees grow to 6–20 m (20–66 ft) tall. The leaves are alternate, 5–11 cm (2.0–4.3 in) long and 2–4 cm (0.79–1.6 in) broad, with a short acuminate apex and an entire margin. The flowers are yellowish-green, produced in an [umbel](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Umbel "Umbel"); the fruit is a woody capsule 2.5–3 cm (0.98–1.2 in) long.

The genus is best known, together with *[Gyrinops](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Gyrinops "Gyrinops")*, as the principal producer of the [resin](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Resin "Resin") -suffused [agarwood](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Agarwood "Agarwood") used in aromatic incense production, especially *[Aquilaria malaccensis](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Aquilaria_malaccensis "Aquilaria malaccensis")*.[^2] [^3] The depletion of wild trees from indiscriminate cutting for agarwood has resulted in the trees being listed and protected as an [endangered species](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Endangered_species "Endangered species").[^2] [^3] [^4] Projects are currently underway in some countries in southeast Asia to infect cultivated *Aquilaria* trees artificially to produce agarwood in a sustainable manner.[^4] In Indonesia, for example, there have been proposals to encourage the planting of gahara, as it is known locally, in eastern Indonesia, particularly in the province of [Papua](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Papua_\(province\) "Papua (province)").[^5]
